ALA Sandbox

16 February, 2012

By John Tann, Atlas of Living Australia

The ALA Sandbox can be used for a variety of tasks. One really useful and yet simple task is detailed below. More examples later …

Checking a list of names

The ALA Sandbox will allow you to view your data with ALA tools. Without making your data public, you can upload data as lists and see it presented on a map or bar chart, including a record-by-record analysis.

One simple use of the Sandbox is to check a list of scientific names to see whether the Atlas has a match in its species lists. Data quality tests will show up spelling and other errors, and present the latest name from the National Species Lists for each organism.

Step 1

List of scientific names from a spreadsheet

Highlight a column of names in a spreadsheet.
Copy and paste the names into http://sandbox.ala.org.au/datacheck/

In the image to the left, Eragostis curvula is actually a misspelling of Eragrostis curvula.

Note: Sandbox works best in Firefox, Chrome or Safari. An Internet Explorer version is coming.

Step 2

Column Header Names

Sandbox will attempt to interpret the header and first few lines. You might need to rename the header to ‘scientificName’.

Adjust and ‘Reprocess sample’ if required.

Step 3

Dataset Name

Give your dataset a name, and ‘Upload’.

Step 4

Occurrence records

Follow the link to your uploaded dataset. Your data can now be presented in many ways. For example, look for ‘Record Issues’. Adjust and re-run as necessary.

You can download a CSV file that can be opened as a spreadsheet, and will include latest names and data check results.

Beware: The Sandbox is experimental. It can at times be a bit flaky, but it will change and get better at any time.
