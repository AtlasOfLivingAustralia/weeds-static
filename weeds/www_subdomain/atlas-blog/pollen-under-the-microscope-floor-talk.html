7 October, 2011 By Robyn Lawrence

Exhibition

“Pollen under the Microscope”, see pollen up close and … at this amazing exhibition!

6 – 18 October 2011
CSIRO Discovery, Clunies Ross Street, Canberra
Industry Link Room, Gallery space

Floor Talk

Missed the exhibition launch of the Pollen Under the Microscope?

Nil desperandum? (Latin) You still have an opportunity to experience it!

Exhibition Floor Talk

Tuesday 11 October at 12.30pm
CSIRO Discovery, Canberra
Industry Link Room, Gallery space

Free, no bookings required.

Join one of CSIRO’s palynologists (pollen scientists) discussing the exhibition and expounding on all things pollen …

Andrew Thornhill from the Centre for Australian National Biodiversity Research (CANBR), CSIRO Division of Plant Industry, is leading the floor talk for this fascinating exhibition.

Andrew recently submitted his PhD thesis on the pollen of Australia’s Myrtaceae family - a no mean feat!

Suzette, maker of the Acacia tree.
You too, can take your piccie here.

General Information

This exhibition celebrates both the art and science of pollination. From flowering plant reproduction and the roles of pollinators such as the birds and the bees, to modern microscopes revealing the stunning beauty of pollen. These microscopes are helping scientists record the species that exist today and could aid in the discovery of new species.

The exhibition “Pollen Under the Microscope” celebrates the purchase of the Classifynder™ light microscope and image detection system, developed by Massey University in New Zealand. The Classifynder helps to automatically identify pollen grains, thereby speeding up our understanding of nature.
