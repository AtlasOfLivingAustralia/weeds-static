International Barcode of Life Conference presentations now online

6 January, 2012

The International Barcode of Life Conference in Adelaide 28 November - 3 December 2011 was a great opportunity to meet colleagues from across the world and hear about the latest international research with 463 participants from 61 countries, 504 abstracts submitted from 62 countries and 379 presentations from 49 countries.

The Conference presentations are now on-line. Session Agendas have been updated and now include links to audio recordings of plenary and taxon plenary presentations, and powerpoint files and poster presentations in PDF. Questions (or problems) can be sent to cbol@si.edu

CBOL is eager to have your feedback on the Adelaide Conference and your advice for the next international conference. Please provide your input on the Connect Discussion Forum.

Policy Discussions in Adelaide

Construction of the global reference library is a core objective of the barcoding community and two policy issues related to the library were discussed actively in Adelaide.

  • Barcode Data Release

     The presentation by Scott Miller, CBOL’s Chair, and others pointed out that many, perhaps a majority, of barcode records have not yet been released on BOLD or published on GenBank. According to a survey of presenters at the Adelaide Conference, only 25% of the responding presenters have released the data associated with their presentations. The International Barcode of Life project (iBOL) and CBOL are both promoting earlier data release. iBOL is already accelerating its release of complete records and CBOL published a data release announcement in ZooKeys during the Conference.

  • Barcode Data Standard

     CBOL’s Executive Committee met on Sunday, 4 December, immediately after the Conference. They reviewed compliance with the BARCODE data standard and found significant room for improvement concerning the standard’s requirements for a taxonomic name and a formatted link to voucher specimens. The Committee has asked GenBank to work with BOLD to bring records into compliance in the coming months. CBOL is also working with BOLD to create valid voucher IDs using data from biorepositories.org.

Barcode Highlights 2011

Printed copies of Barcode Highlights 2011, produced jointly by CBOL and iBOL, were distributed during the Adelaide conference and the brochure is now available on the web. If you need printed copies for distribution, please write to cbol@si.edu.
