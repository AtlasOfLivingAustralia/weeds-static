The Atlas of Living Australia has been working in the citizen science area since 2009.

In early 2009 the Atlas partnered with the Earthwatch Institute to deliver the newly formed Climatewatch project.  This original software won two spatial industry awards for Gaia Resources.

In late 2010 the Atlas commissioned a review of the functionality of Citizen Science web sites in Australia, with a focus on some specifically chosen partner sites.  The review identified a lot more work was being done in this space and the final report will shortly be made publicly available.

Biodiversity Snapshot's mobile design

We have been working on developing an open source software package called the Biological Data Recording System (more on that over on the Citizen Science tools pages).  

In August 2010 the first release of the new software occurred with the launch of Museum Victoria’s  web site Biodiversity Snapshots. Along with the wealth of educational materials that are included from Museum Victoria this represents the first production system including an HTML 5 based mobile application enabling data to be recorded in the field using iPhones and Android based phones - even when you are offline!

In the leadup to the main release of the Atlas of Living Australia late in 2010 we also worked with Birds Australia to develop their own citizen science portal which they administer and manage.  This initially started with the Carnaby’s Black-Cockatoo Recovery Project.  The team migrated data from a UWA research project (with over 500 users and 3,000 records) from a student project to the implementation that is now in production (and in April, 2011, clocked over 1,000 users and 4,000 records).

At the moment working with a range of new BDRS implementations including Queensland Museum, TERN‘s Ausplots program, and a range of the National Collections under the auspices of TRIN.  Other portals are in development for Myrtle Rust, Bushblitz and the Great Eastern Ranges so the next few months are going to be a very busy period.

We have a lot more to go with Citizen Science in the next year so stay tuned for more information as it happens.
