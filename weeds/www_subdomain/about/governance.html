Project Background

The Atlas of Living Australia (ALA) project was established and is funded through the National Collaborative Research Infrastructure Strategy National Collaborative Research Infrastructure Strategy (NCRIS) and further supported by the Super Science Initiative funded from the Education Investment Fund.

Original funding was obtained from the Commonwealth Department of Education, Science and Training (DEST), and now is administered through the Department of Innovation, Industry, Science and Research (DIISR).

In addition, the Atlas partners are making substantial and essential in-kind contributions.

The Atlas project is a partnership between the Commonwealth Scientific and Industrial Research Organisation, museums, herbaria and other biological collections and their Councils of Heads, two universities and the Australian Government.

The Australian Government Department of Sustainability, Environment, Water, Population and Communities and the Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry are two key Atlas partners, being responsible for a huge amount of Australia’s biological and sustainability knowledge and management.

Atlas partners have expertise in and responsibility for identifying, collecting and preserving Australia’s biological collections, research, literature, history, specimens, data, information and records.

The Atlas seeks to integrate rich biological data from all its partners, and to provide tools for sharing, synthesis and analysis of these data.

Management Committee

The Management Committee was established to provide strategic guidance for CSIRO and the ALA Director and to represent and engage with key partners, data providers and the overall collection and user community. The ALA Management Committee consists of representatives from CSIRO, the peak bodies representing Australian natural history collections, DSEWPaC and three related NCRIS capabilities (TERN, ABIN, APN). The current chair is John Hooper (Council of Heads of Australian Faunal Collections -CHAFC).

More about the ALA Management Committee »

Project Steering Committee

Following the allocation of the Education Investment Fund (EIF) funding in 2008, a new ALA Project Steering Committee was established with representation from CSIRO, the ALA Management Committee, DIISR and DSEWPaC (as a key stakeholder). As the lead agency, CSIRO has the responsibility to manage the ALA project and EIF funds and ensure that the project is conducted in accordance with the obligations under the DIISR Funding Agreement.

More about the ALA Steering Committee »

IBS Steering Committee

The NCRIS Integrated Biological Systems (IBS) capability comprises three separate but related projects – the Australian Phenomics Network, the National Plant Phenomics Facility and The Atlas of Living Australia.  The IBS Committee provides overall strategic vision to, and integration between, the capability components.

More about the ALA IBS Steering Committee »

Technical Scoping Group

The ALA Technical Scoping Group comprises a number of experts in biodiversity informatics who have provided valuable guidance on technical issues for the Atlas.
