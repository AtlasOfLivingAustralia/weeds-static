New ID tools for rainforest plants and environmental weeds now online

20 March, 2012

Rainforest key in IdentifyLife

Identification and information tools for rainforest plants and environmental weeds are now online at ‘Keys Central’ section of IdentifyLife at www.identifylife.org

Australian Tropical Rainforest Plants – http://www.anbg.gov.au/cpbr/cd-keys/rfk/index.html

This key is an interactive identification and information system for 2154 species of trees, shrubs and vines of northern Australian rain forests. New modules are being developed on orchids, herbs and ferns. This key was developed over 40 years by a team including CSIRO, the Australian Tropical Herbarium, the Reef and Rainforest Research Centre and the Federal Government.

Environmental Weeds of Australia – http://www.business.qld.gov.au/agriculture/land-management/weed-identification-tool.html

This online invasive weed identification resource to over 1,000 weed species was developed at The University of Queensland with support from the Cooperative Research Centre for Weed Management with funding from Biosecurity Queensland.

You can access a multitude of identification keys and tools from the Keys Central section on the IdentifyLife site. The IdentifyLife team at The University of Queensland and Western Australian Herbarium are developing a global platform for developing, storing and sharing descriptive data for the world’s organisms. The IdentifyLife project also aims to develop an online identification tool, a “Key to All Life”.

If you have any queries or would like more information about IdentifyLife, please contact Audrey Jinks on A.Jinks@uq.edu.au
