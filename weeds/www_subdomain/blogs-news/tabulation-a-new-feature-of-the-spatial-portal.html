Tabulation - a new feature of the Spatial Portal

21 February, 2012

New Features of the Spatial Portal

The latest release of the Spatial Portal has many new and exciting features such as tabulation, mapping facets (species attributes), mapping multiple layers at once, a new points to grid matrix tool, and a standard way of using species assemblages in one layer, and more.

This blog gives you a ‘taste of tabulation’. In postings to come, we will demonstrate other new features.

Tabulation is the contextual layer counterpart to Scatterplots. In scatterplots, the X and Y axes are environmental layers (e.g., temperature, rainfall) and the points on the graph are occurrences. With tabulation, we use contextual layers (e.g., land use, States and Territories) and the X and Y axes are rows and columns of a table and the entries in the table cells can be any one of the following 12 values:

  1. Area of intersection (km2)
  2. Percentage of area (a)
  3. Percentage of row total (a)
  4. Percentage of column total (a)
  5. Number of species of taxa/assemblage
  6. Percentage of species of taxa/assemblage (e)
  7. Percentage of row total of (e)
  8. Percentage of column total of (e)
  9. Number of occurrences of selected taxa
  10. Percentage of occurrences of selected taxa (i)
  11. Percentage of row total of (i)
  12. Percentage of column total of (i)

On the ‘Tools menu, select ‘Tabulate’.

Tabulation Step1 chosen values

The example is for Species richness (%) tabulated across ‘Australian States and Territories’ for ‘Land cover (class’). The 1.9% is the first row and column of the table is interpreted as 1.9% of species richness in South Australia occurs in ‘Native shrublands and heathlands’. (Click the image below to enlarge.)
